# Ancient Hostile Force

## Status

- Canonical world-level threat document
- Hidden background force, not active public-story surface

## Core identity

- A former foe of the ancients still exists in a weakened state
- It is not strong enough anymore to dominate large regions of the world directly
- Its influence now works through individuals, bargains, corruption, and selective empowerment
- It wants the ancients' history erased from the world

## Current capability

- Can still contact or influence vulnerable power-seekers
- Can make targeted bargains with rulers and ambitious intermediaries
- Can grant limited supernatural benefit when doing so serves its longer aim
- Cannot currently exercise broad world-controlling force on its own

## Relationship to the crystal

- The crystal is central to its current strategy
- It wants the crystal acquired through human agents rather than by its own open return
- It uses political desperation and personal weakness to move other powers toward that goal

## Relationship to the opposing kingdom

- The opposing kingdom was originally part of the same older kingdom as Leo's side
- A long-ago conflict between brothers split that older kingdom into two realms
- The opposing kingdom is under pressure from this force
- The force is the same larger power previously noted as wanting the ancients' history wiped out
- Its pressure helps explain the kingdom's desperation and long sabotage campaign

## The opposing king's bargain

- The king of the opposing kingdom is the uncle of the king on Leo's side
- The king of the opposing kingdom made a deal with this force
- In exchange for the crystal, he was promised restored youth
- The force granted that youth early as an incentive and as preparation for the coming seizure of the crystal
- He is in truth quite old, but now appears as a strong young nobleman
- The restored body gives him the strength, cunning, and physical credibility to pursue the plan more effectively

## Story function

- Expands the crystal conflict beyond ordinary interstate rivalry
- Reveals that both kingdoms are being shaped by pressures older than their current politics
- Lets the larger threat work through intimate corruption rather than immediate apocalyptic spectacle

## Reveal handling

- This force should remain background truth until the story is ready to widen beyond kingdom-level corruption
- Early material should prioritize political consequence, religious distortion, and hidden bargains over explicit cosmological exposition
