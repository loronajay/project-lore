# Aldric

## Status

- Canonical character file
- Major recurring character; antagonist role develops through later story events

## Story role

- Current General of Eden's army
- Gideon's most trusted instrument
- Becomes a primary recurring threat to the protagonist party as later developments place him in direct opposition to them

## Public identity

- Known throughout the army and kingdom for exceptional combat ability
- Rose through the ranks on merit — word of his prowess spread before Gideon ever tested him directly
- Passed every test Gideon put to him and was immediately promoted to General
- His promotion came after Richard's exile
- Around early 40s in Act 2

## Core character

- Purely loyal to Gideon with no question or hesitation
- Fully inhabits Gideon's version of reality — not a cynic serving power, a true believer operating inside the lie
- Sharp and observant in a way that sets him apart; catches things most people miss entirely
- Once he locks on to a target or objective, nothing stops him
- His danger comes from competence and conviction, not cruelty or personal ambition

## Relationship to the lie

- Has no awareness of the deeper truth behind Gideon's rule
- His loyalty is clean and sincere from the inside — Gideon tested him, trusted him, elevated him
- He has no reason to question a king who has only ever given him the chance to prove himself and rewarded him for it
- The moral failure is not visible to him from where he stands

## Relationships

- **Gideon**: king, the man who recognized and elevated him; his complete frame of reference for legitimate authority
- **Leo**: sent by Gideon to retrieve Leo after Esmeralda's report; finds him unconscious and immediately recognizes the royal army insignia on Jack's sword — that recognition is institutional, not personal; he knows the sword's military significance, not the bloodline attached to it; becomes a recurring antagonist encounter through later story events
- **Richard**: preceded him as General

## Locked canon

- Has several encounters with the protagonist party across the story
- Do not introduce as an antagonist from the start — his opposition develops through later events, not from his first appearance
