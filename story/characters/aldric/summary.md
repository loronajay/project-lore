# Aldric Summary

Aldric is the most dangerous kind of obstacle: competent, loyal, and completely wrong about the thing that matters most. He did not buy his way to General or inherit it through politics. He earned it. Word of his combat ability spread through the ranks before Gideon ever summoned him, and when Gideon tested him, Aldric passed everything put in front of him. The promotion was immediate. That history matters because it means Aldric has no grievance, no suppressed doubt, and no gap between the version of the world Gideon gave him and the version he lives inside. He is not serving power cynically. He is serving what he genuinely believes is a legitimate king who recognized him, trusted him, and gave him the chance to prove what he was.

That sincerity is part of what makes him effective. He is sharp in the specific way that unsettles people trying to hide something - he notices what others walk past, catches inconsistencies most people smooth over without registering, and follows threads that weaker observers would drop. Once he locks on to a target or objective, nothing stops him. He first appears when Gideon sends him to retrieve Leo after Esmeralda's report. He finds the boy unconscious outside the castle walls and recognizes the royal army insignia on the sword immediately. That recognition is institutional, not personal - he knows what the sword means in the context of Eden's military history, not in the context of the bloodline attached to it.

At that point he is simply a general doing his job. The antagonist role develops later, through story events that bring him into direct and sustained opposition with the protagonist party. When that happens, his qualities stop being background texture and become the central problem: a man certain enough in his purpose to keep coming, perceptive enough to close every gap, and loyal enough that nothing short of the full truth could ever turn him. He does not yet have reason to look at the foundation beneath what he is serving. The tragedy of his position is that the very competence that makes him dangerous also means that if he ever did see it clearly, he would be one of the most formidable people alive on either side.
