# Rowan / Jack (Leo's Father)

## Status

- Canonical character file
- Major absent presence in the story

## Story role

- Dead father whose legacy shapes Leo's fear, grief, and inheritance
- Former royal army member whose past reaches into the village's present
- Suppressed rightful heir whose hidden history anchors the deeper political conflict

## Public perception

- In healthier years, remembered as respected, outgoing, and deeply helpful
- In exile, known as a good neighbor and devoted father
- Remembered as highly skilled and battle-tested
- During the sickness, increasingly associated with secrecy, distance, and unease

## Present story meaning

- Great father whose deepest attachment in life is Leo
- Contracted the sickness
- Resisted it longer than expected
- Became isolated and distant before death
- Became mysterious largely because the sickness hollowed out an otherwise warm and social man
- Never pitied himself as much as he pitied what Leo was losing

## Inner reality

- Deeply father-oriented
- Kind and respected beneath the harder surface created by illness
- Would have made a great king in temperament as well as lineage

## Screenplay presence

- Appears only through memory in Act 1; never present in the current timeline
- Healthy memory read: upright, wide-shouldered, moving through the market with easy confidence; greeting people by name, laughing, taking up space because the world is good and there is room; his free hand settles briefly on young Leo's head without looking down, not a word, just a hand
- Sick memory read: pale, thinner, eyes open but with a quality that doesn't look restful; awake the way a person is awake when sleep has stopped being available to them; voice slow and low; the effort of small movement visible
- Training alone (seen through a curtain gap by young Leo): not practicing, something more native than practice; loose at the shoulder, weight low and shifting, the sword's edge always oriented somewhere that seems obvious only after the fact; not fighting anything, but watching him you would think there was something real in front of him
- The fight with Marcus (seen through Leo's memory): moves out of the way the way water moves around a stone, without effort, without drama, already somewhere else before the fist arrives; the counter is precise and fast with nothing improvised about it; the fight ends quickly with Marcus on the ground
- In the fight memory, Jack says something back to Marcus quiet, not backing up; Marcus's posture says he has wanted this for a long time

## Buried history

- Born Rowan; renamed Jack by the King when erasing his royal identity, a deliberate act of replacing a name with weight with something plain and unremarkable
- Leo knows him only as Jack; Rowan is buried with everything else about who his father truly was
- Raised from infancy under a false commoner identity, believing himself an orphan the King took in
- Grew up loving the King as a father figure and benefactor; served him with complete loyalty because he knew nothing of the betrayal beneath his life
- The King's betrayal of him goes back to infancy
- Was internally confirmed as the true gene-bearer in his generation and the strongest legitimate claimant to the throne under the kingdom's own belief system
- The King ordered him, Marcus, and his squadron to wipe out another exiled village, erase its history, and retrieve the crystal
- Once he understood the truth of the mission, he refused, aborted it, and returned
- The King exiled the squadron and secretly executed everyone except Jack and Marcus
- Initially met Richard's account of his true identity with disbelief
- Feared leaving Leo without a father after Leo's mother was already gone; that fear was his primary resistance to accepting the rebellion
- Marcus was brought into the same rebellion plan but refused it, helping drive the later fracture and fight between him and Jack
- Eventually accepted the truth and got on board with the cause
- Was being prepared by Richard for a rebellion intended to claim justice and the throne
- Abandoned that path when the sickness took hold

## Combat identity

- Exceptionally skilled fighter
- Loose, fluid, non-traditional style
- Considered the most skilled by those who knew him
- His movement feels instinctive, refined, and difficult to imitate

## Relationships

- **Leo**: beloved son, close bond before illness, source of inheritance and grief after his decline; Leo was the greatest joy in his life
- **Richard**: longtime comrade, close friend, and later secret collaborator in a larger cause
- **Marcus**: former comrade, later violent opponent
- **The King**: hidden older brother, false benefactor, the father-figure bond he grew up inside, and hidden usurper

## Important objects

- His sword - held by Richard after Jack's death; Richard decided to give it to Leo but was murdered before acting on it, so the sword does not reach Leo through Richard's hand
- Hidden weapons cache kept in his room; Leo did not know it existed

## Locked canon

- The King is his older brother; keep out of active-story material until deliberately unlocked
- He is the true gene-bearer and the legitimate heir under the kingdom's own rules; keep out of active-story material until deliberately unlocked

- His room remains emotionally off-limits to Leo in the active story
