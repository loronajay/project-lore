This document is a fast-reference bridge between character canon and chronology.

Use it when:
- an agent needs to remember who is tied to whom
- a writing pass depends on who knows what
- the story timeline is getting hard to hold in working memory

## Core spine

At the center of the backstory is one theft:
- Gideon stole legitimacy
- Jack was the rightful line he buried
- Richard eventually discovered enough of that theft to resist it
- Leo is the surviving consequence of the buried line
- Esmeralda is Gideon's fabricated public answer to the same legitimacy problem

Almost every major relationship in the story branches off that theft.

## Relationship map

### Leo

- **Jack**: Leo's father; loved deeply; source of grief, warmth, and hidden inheritance
- **Mother**: dead since Leo was very young; survives in memory as tenderness and in the jewelry box as the last physical keepsake
- **Richard**: distant protector bound by promise to Jack; helps Leo indirectly and avoids stepping into an overt father role
- **Marshe**: recurring bully; personal threat long before the Richard murder events
- **Marcus**: Marshe's father; part of the older conflict Leo only understands through fragments
- **Gideon**: unknown true family tie in early story; later manipulates Leo by weaponizing Jack's memory
- **Esmeralda**: later becomes a genuine close bond under Gideon's arrangement

### Jack / Rowan

- **Gideon**: older brother, hidden from Jack for most of his life; false benefactor, usurper, and architect of Jack's erased identity
- **Leo**: beloved son and emotional center of Jack's later life
- **Leo's mother**: love found in exile; gives Jack the family life that becomes more important to him than ambition
- **Richard**: closest comrade and later collaborator in the buried rebellion
- **Marcus**: former comrade whose envy and resentment later harden into fracture
- **Marshe**: son of Marcus; Jack's victory over Marcus becomes part of Marshe's inherited hatred

### Richard

- **Jack**: friend, comrade, and later chosen political future
- **Leo**: watches over him after Jack's death; torn between promise and fear that Leo is the last viable future
- **Gideon**: former ruler and superior he served before fully understanding the scale of the lie
- **Esmeralda's mother**: lost love; the relationship Gideon unknowingly stole from him
- **Esmeralda**: Richard's biological daughter; loved from afar; hidden from both her and Gideon
- **Marcus**: former comrade Richard later kills to stop an elder attack
- **Marshe**: son of Marcus; later murders Richard after learning Richard killed his father

### Gideon

- **Jack / Rowan**: younger brother and true heir Gideon erased, renamed, and weaponized
- **Leo**: Jack's surviving son; later seen by Gideon as useful, controllable, and dangerous
- **Esmeralda**: public daughter and manufactured prophecy answer; Gideon grows genuinely attached to her despite the lie underneath
- **Richard**: once-useful asset who became too dangerous to keep near power
- **Dorian**: rival king and family enemy working from the other side of the split kingdom history
- **Ancient hostile force**: not Gideon's ally in the same way it is Dorian's, but its pressure and the broader crystal struggle shape the field he is trying to dominate

### Esmeralda

- **Gideon**: public father, real captor-benefactor, emotional reality of her upbringing
- **Richard**: biological father unknown to her
- **Mother**: dead under a false public story
- **Leo**: later meaningful bond; one of the few relationships in her life with less performance attached to it

### Marshe

- **Marcus**: father; the loss around him shapes Marshe's life even before he knows the full truth
- **Richard**: the man who killed his father, though Marshe only learns that later
- **Leo**: target of longstanding resentment, bullying, and later lethal collision
- **Jack**: Marshe witnessed Marcus lose to Jack as a child; that memory helps feed his fixation on Leo

### Marcus

- **Jack**: comrade turned rival; resents Jack's rank, decisions, and moral authority
- **Richard**: former comrade who initially hoped Marcus might stand with the rebellion
- **Marshe**: son left behind after Marcus's death at Richard's hands

## Faction map

### The buried legitimate line

- Jack is the true heir under the kingdom's own succession logic
- Leo is the hidden continuation of that line
- Richard becomes steward of that buried future after learning the truth

### The ruling false line

- Gideon rules through suppression, propaganda, and doctrinal control
- Esmeralda is positioned as his manufactured continuity plan
- Aldric later serves as the loyal enforcement arm of Gideon's order

### The external pressure line

- Dorian wants the crystal and operates through long sabotage rather than direct war alone
- The ancient hostile force wants the ancients' legacy erased and deploys corruption, bargains, and the sickness

## Knowledge map

Use this section to prevent accidental reveal drift.

### What Leo knows in early Act 1

- his father is dead
- Richard is strange and frightening but has helped him
- Marshe is a real threat
- the village is deteriorating
- people call the sickness a curse
- his mother's jewelry box is emotionally sacred

### What Leo does not know in early Act 1

- Jack's real name is Rowan
- Gideon is Jack's brother
- Jack was the rightful heir
- Richard was preparing rebellion around Jack
- Richard is Esmeralda's biological father
- the sickness was deliberately introduced
- Leo himself matters to the larger succession struggle

### What Richard knows by the time the film starts

- Gideon's public legitimacy is false
- Jack was the buried rightful line
- Leo may be the last surviving path back to that future
- Esmeralda is his daughter
- the village is dangerous in ways the public does not understand

### What Richard still does not know for certain

- the full outer strategy of Dorian and the hostile force
- whether Leo will ever need to be brought into the conflict
- whether preserving Jack's promise is still morally possible

### What Gideon knows by the time the film starts

- Jack was the true threat he failed to bury cleanly
- Richard knows too much
- Esmeralda must be manufactured into legitimacy
- the hidden doctrinal marker would expose him if it surfaced

### What Gideon does not yet fully understand

- the scale of Leo's future significance
- Leo's possible relation to the stronger future-facing prophecy
- Richard's biological tie to Esmeralda

## Working timeline

This is not day-precise. It is organized by story era so agents can place events quickly.

### Era 1: split kingdom and first corruption

- the older kingdom splits into Eden and Thorne after a brothers' conflict
- Gideon's father retains Eden
- Dorian leads Thorne
- Dorian is later approached by the ancient hostile force and accepts restored youth in exchange for helping secure the crystal
- war between Eden and Thorne follows
- Gideon's father is killed in a wartime assassination

### Era 2: Gideon's false rise

- Gideon is manipulated while young by traitors and pressure around succession
- Rowan is born later as the younger royal child
- Gideon learns he is not the true gene-bearer of his generation
- a deeper doctrinal marker surfaces: the true prophesied figure should read the ancients' language at a glance
- Gideon cannot do this and suppresses the doctrine
- when Rowan is confirmed internally as the true bearer, Gideon chooses murder and erasure
- he has his remaining close family eliminated, seizes power, and is crowned
- he renames Rowan as Jack and raises him under a false commoner identity
- he begins rewriting history and controlling religion

### Era 3: military years and buried loyalties

- Richard joins the army
- Marcus and Jack later serve in the same military world
- Richard rises to General
- Jack proves himself in battle and grows close to Richard
- Richard loses his wife
- Richard later falls in love with the woman who will become Esmeralda's mother
- Gideon promotes Jack to General over Marcus
- Marcus's resentment deepens

### Era 4: massacre refusal and exile

- Gideon sends Jack, Marcus, and their squadron to destroy another exiled village and retrieve the crystal
- Jack realizes the order is wrong and aborts the mission
- Marcus wants to obey
- they return expecting punishment but not the truth of it
- Jack and Marcus are sentenced to exile
- the rest of the squadron is secretly executed
- the exile terms include a strict village weapons ban

### Era 5: Esmeralda's birth and Richard's fall

- Gideon has Richard's love taken to court to bear him a child
- Jack builds a new life in exile and falls in love there
- Esmeralda and Leo are born within months of each other
- Richard knows Esmeralda is his daughter
- Richard and Esmeralda's mother plan to flee with the baby
- Richard pulls back, fearing exposure will kill them all
- she tries alone, is caught, and is secretly executed
- Gideon begins shaping Esmeralda into a public prophecy answer
- Richard's suspicions about Gideon deepen into a clearer picture
- Gideon notices Richard's dangerous proximity to too much truth and exiles him as well

### Era 6: sickness, rebellion attempt, and village fracture

- by the time Richard reaches the village, the sickness is already beginning in subtle form
- Richard hides a forge, begins building weapons, and maintains secret contacts
- a researcher contact helps Richard understand Jack's real identity and Gideon's theft more fully
- Richard brings Jack and Marcus into the truth
- Jack resists at first out of disbelief and fear for Leo
- Marcus is asked to join the rebellion and ultimately refuses it
- Jack and Marcus later fight over the fracture between them
- Leo's mother falls ill and later dies while Leo is still very young
- Marcus, already affected by the sickness, attacks an elder during a council event
- Richard kills Marcus to stop him
- the village sees the gore, not the rescue, and Richard becomes a feared outcast
- Marshe grows up under the shadow of that loss without knowing the full truth
- Jack finally accepts the rebellion cause
- then Jack contracts the sickness, collapsing the future Richard had been building toward
- Jack makes Richard promise to keep Leo out of that conflict

### Era 7: decline before the film

- years pass
- Gideon keeps refining public truth and strengthening Esmeralda's false legitimacy
- Jack weakens slowly but remains deeply attached to Leo
- Jack eventually dies when Leo is around thirteen
- the village mourns Jack openly
- Richard watches from a distance rather than joining openly
- Marshe's bullying of Leo intensifies after Jack is gone
- Richard continues helping Leo indirectly for years without exposing himself

### Era 8: film opening position

- Leo is about sixteen
- poor, isolated, grieving, and trying to survive
- the sickness is becoming more visible in village life
- Richard is still watching him from a distance
- Richard has not yet reached a settled decision on whether Leo must eventually be shaped into the larger conflict
- Marshe is already in the early stages of the sickness
- Esmeralda is living publicly as Princess and as Gideon's manufactured succession answer
- Act 1 opens with Leo's battlefield dream

## Causality shortcuts

Use these when checking whether a scene's subtext still tracks.

- Gideon erased Jack, so Leo grows up in ignorance
- Richard learned too late, so his protection is compromised and indirect
- Marcus resented Jack, so Marshe inherits a damaged emotional field before he even learns the truth
- Esmeralda is a manufactured answer to the same legitimacy crisis Leo unknowingly embodies from the other side
- the sickness does not just create danger; it accelerates unresolved grief, fear, and violence already present in the village

## Agent use note

When drafting scenes, ask three quick questions:
- what is publicly believed here?
- what is privately true here?
- which relationships are active in the scene even if only one character is physically present?
