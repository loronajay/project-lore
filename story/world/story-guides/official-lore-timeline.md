# Official Lore Timeline

This is the cleaner markdown reference version of the official canon timeline.

Use it when:
- an agent needs chronological order quickly
- a writing pass depends on when a relationship changes
- timeline logic needs to be checked without parsing the numbered `.txt`

Primary purpose:
- preserve chronology
- make cause and effect easier to scan
- reduce timeline drift during collaborative writing

Status:
- this is the primary working chronology file
- update this file first when canon chronology changes

The numbered source timeline still exists in:
- `story/world/story-guides/official-lore-timeline.txt`

The `.txt` file is a legacy mirror kept for compatibility with older notes and workflows.
If the two ever drift, treat this markdown file as the primary source and reconcile the `.txt` intentionally.

## Era 1: Kingdom split and early war

- A brothers' feud over rights fractures the older kingdom.
- The kingdom splits into Eden and Thorne.
- Gideon's father retains Eden.
- Dorian takes those still loyal to him and leaves some behind to spy inside Eden.
- Dorian builds Thorne into its own kingdom.
- A strange hostile entity approaches Dorian because it also wants Eden to fall.
- Dorian accepts a bargain: restored youth in exchange for helping secure the crystal.
- War breaks out between Eden and Thorne.
- Gideon is born shortly before the first war between the two kingdoms.
- During the early war, Gideon's father is assassinated in a wartime attack, leaving the queen to rule Eden.

## Era 2: Gideon's manipulation and seizure of power

- Gideon is being targeted for manipulation while still young.
- Rowan is born later as the younger royal child.
- Gideon learns he is not the true gene-bearer of his generation.
- Around the same time, a deeper doctrinal truth surfaces inside the castle: the true prophesied figure should be able to read the ancients' language at a glance.
- Gideon cannot fulfill that marker and immediately suppresses it.
- Gideon decides to undercut the Thorne spies and take over the crystal plot for himself.
- When Rowan is internally confirmed as the true bearer, Gideon decides murdering the rest of his family is still the best option.
- Gideon becomes officially crowned.
- He immediately starts setting his plan into motion by giving Rowan a new identity: Jack.
- Over time, Gideon carefully revises history through paid-off servants and priests.
- While rewriting the past, he also shapes Jack into the ultimate soldier.

## Era 3: Army years and shifting loyalties

- Richard joins the army.
- Marcus joins around the same time Jack does, and the two develop a friendly rivalry.
- Battles continue between Eden and Thorne over the years.
- Jack participates in some of the later battles alongside Richard and Marcus.
- Jack shows major promise and composure in battle.
- Richard, already notably older than Jack, also distinguishes himself.
- Richard, Marcus, Jack, and some key others survive a battle where many others do not.
- Richard is promoted to General to replace the fallen General before him.
- Richard and Jack develop a bond on and off the battlefield.
- Richard loses his wife.
- Jack is there to console him.
- Later, Richard meets Esmeralda's mother and the two begin a secret relationship.
- Marshe is born.
- Gideon notices Richard losing focus and promotes Jack to General instead.
- Marcus is upset that he now works under Jack.

## Era 4: The massacre order and exile event

- Jack is sent to wipe out a village of supposed rebels.
- Jack realizes something is wrong and hesitates.
- He argues with Marcus about aborting the mission.
- Jack uses his authority to turn the squadron back toward the castle.
- Upon returning, Jack and Marcus are sentenced to exile.
- The night before their escort, everyone else in the squadron is executed behind closed doors.
- On arrival in the exile village, the terms of exile are formally stated.
- Those terms include a strict ban on weapons within the village.

## Era 5: Richard sees the lie more clearly

- Richard starts noticing a pattern of inconsistency in the kingdom.
- He asks his researcher friend to look more closely.
- He warns that friend to be extremely careful.
- Out of anger and desperation, Gideon sends a mercenary to acquire the most beautiful woman in the kingdom to bear his child.
- That woman is Richard's new love interest.
- Richard realizes she now sits beside the King and is powerless to stop it.
- At the same time, Jack meets a woman in the exile village and they fall in love.

## Era 6: Leo and Esmeralda are born into opposite lives

- Esmeralda and Leo are expected to be born within a few months of each other.
- Esmeralda and Leo are born.
- Richard knows Esmeralda is his daughter because he and her mother had been seeing each other in secret.
- Richard and Esmeralda's mother plan to flee with her together.
- Richard ultimately decides it is too dangerous: exposure would get them all killed, and the castle seems safer for Esmeralda than exile.
- Esmeralda's mother attempts the escape alone.
- She is caught and secretly executed, buried beneath a lie of illness.
- Gideon decides he is running out of time and begins positioning Esmeralda publicly as Princess and, beneath that, as the super modern ancient.
- Richard can do nothing but watch the lie unfold as Esmeralda grows from afar.
- He now understands more clearly how far Gideon is willing to go to manipulate truth.
- Richard begins making outside contacts to prepare rebellion once he has enough proof.
- Gideon notices the way Richard looks at Esmeralda and remembers seeing Richard with her mother.
- Gideon exiles Richard in secret, sending him to the same fate as Jack and Marcus.
- Aldric is appointed as the new General.

## Era 7: The sickness enters the village and rebellion begins to form

- During this same broad period, Dorian receives more pressure from the hostile entity to retrieve the crystal.
- Thorne, Dorian, and the entity learn that Rowan / Jack is in the exile village.
- The entity releases a subtle virus onto the village.
- Shortly after Richard's arrival there, the sickness is already in its proto-stage.
- Richard begins building weapons soon after arriving.
- He constructs a hidden forge and works at night under the public cover of being a carpenter.
- By the time the film starts, he has a sizeable hidden collection.
- Richard receives messages from his researcher friend and learns the truth about Jack's hidden identity.
- He fills Jack and Marcus in.
- Jack is heartbroken and disbelieving because Gideon had once felt like a father figure.
- Richard initially believes Marcus may help in good faith because Marcus is battle-proven and wants his family out of exile.

## Era 8: Delay, fracture, and failed rebellion

- Leo's mother falls ill.
- Her illness delays the rebellion further.
- The King begins to develop a genuine attachment to Esmeralda.
- During this time, Richard continues strengthening his rebellion plan.
- Leo's mother dies while Leo is still a toddler.
- By then Esmeralda is already being raised as Princess and fed lies about her prophetic role.
- Jack still hesitates on joining the rebellion.
- Marcus is brought into the rebellion plan and refuses it outright.
- The argument between Jack and Marcus partly grows out of that fracture.
- Jack and Marcus fight, and Jack wins.

## Era 9: Marcus falls, Marshe is shaped by loss

- A council meeting is underway in the village.
- Marcus, already affected by the illness without anyone realizing it, attacks an elder.
- Richard acts quickly and kills Marcus.
- Richard becomes an outcast because the village sees the violent spectacle and not the full story.
- Young Marshe does not understand what really happened.
- Marshe's mother is left to raise him alone before she later dies of the illness.
- Marshe is eventually left to fend for himself.

## Era 10: Jack accepts the cause, then loses it

- After the fracture with Marcus, Jack finally accepts his role in the rebellion.
- Richard has already been making weapons in secret, and Jack begins training right away.
- Jack contracts the illness and tells Richard.
- Richard's hopes nearly collapse.
- Richard realizes Leo may still carry the gene and begins to fear the boy may be the last remaining path back to the future he and Jack failed to secure.
- Jack makes Richard promise never to let Leo join a conflict like that.
- He wants Leo to have a normal life.
- Richard hesitates, but accepts, partly because he understands the feeling through his hidden daughter.
- Richard settles into the public life of a carpenter while privately carrying the promise to watch over Leo after Jack dies.

## Era 11: Slow decline before the film

- Years pass.
- Both sides of the larger conflict settle into long-term living rather than open confrontation.
- Leo and Esmeralda grow up in radically different worlds.
- Jack continues fighting the illness over time.
- Gideon is still shaping public knowledge and has successfully placed Esmeralda publicly as Princess and in the deeper royal narrative as the prophesied super modern ancient.
- Jack no longer trains and mostly stays indoors.
- He spends as much time with Leo as his body allows.
- Leo is saddened by his father's changed appearance.
- Richard is as well.
- Jack finally dies when Leo is about thirteen.
- The village holds a large funeral for Jack and openly mourns him as a kind, protective, helpful man.
- Richard does not attend openly but watches from nearby in hiding.
- He watches young Leo cry and receive comfort from the village.
- After Jack's death, Marshe begins openly bullying Leo.
- Part of that hatred has been building for years after Marshe watched his father lose a fight to Jack.
- Richard continues helping Leo in unseen ways over the following years.
- He is still not ready to expose himself openly to the boy.
- Esmeralda continues living the life of a princess.
- Leo fends for himself with occasional hidden help from Richard up until the film starts.

## Film opening position

- Leo is about sixteen.
- He is poor, isolated, and still living inside the grief left by Jack's death.
- Richard is watching him from a distance.
- Richard's exact internal shift on whether Leo must eventually be shaped remains unresolved at this stage.
- Marshe's hostility is already established.
- The sickness is increasingly visible in village life.
- Esmeralda is living publicly as Princess inside the King's manufactured royal narrative.
- The film begins with Leo's battlefield dream.

## Fast causality chain

- Gideon suppresses the true succession order.
- Jack grows up loyal to the man who erased him.
- Jack's refusal of the massacre order leads to exile.
- Richard's deeper investigation turns exile into the seed of rebellion.
- Esmeralda's birth becomes Gideon's public succession solution.
- The hostile force deploys the sickness once Jack's bloodline is located in exile.
- Marcus's refusal and infection help fracture the rebellion before it can take hold.
- Richard's killing of Marcus isolates him socially.
- Jack's illness collapses the rebellion completely.
- Leo grows up in the ruins of a future that almost existed.
