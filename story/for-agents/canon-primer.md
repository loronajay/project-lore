# Canon Primer

Fast-load reference for session start. Authoritative sources are the individual docs; this is a shortcut, not a replacement.

---

## World

- **Eden** - medieval kingdom ruled by Gideon (dictator, carefully managed benevolent image). Authority via propaganda, altered records, weaponized religion.
- **Exile village** - outskirts of Eden; populated by exiles and their descendants; deteriorating; under a weapons ban enforced by occasional royal soldier audits.
- **Thorne** - rival kingdom ruled by Dorian (Gideon's uncle). Formerly one kingdom with Eden, split by a brothers' conflict. An early open war between the two kingdoms - hard-fought on both sides, and the war during which Gideon's father was killed in a wartime assassination - ended without clean resolution. After that, Thorne shifted strategy: rather than press toward the crystal through conflict it couldn't guarantee winning, it spent decades studying Eden's succession logic and internal fractures, placing traitors in the court and running a long sabotage campaign from the inside.
- **Ancient hostile force** - weakened former foe of the ancients. Works through individual bargains and corruption. Wants the ancients' history erased. Introduced the sickness into the village deliberately once it learned Jack's bloodline was there. Remains offscreen and indirect in early material.

---

## Religion and doctrine

- Kingdom religion teaches descent from the ancients; each generation produces a modern ancient meant to rule.
- A **future-facing prophecy** says a stronger figure will one day lead the people back to the ancients.
- **Hidden doctrine** (suppressed): the true prophesied figure can read the ancients' language at a glance. Gideon cannot. He suppresses this fact.
- Only Gideon and his tight propaganda inner circle have access to real ancient material. The broader clergy and scholars receive curated "findings."
- The prophecy itself is real. Gideon does not rewrite it - he manufactures Esmeralda's claim to fulfill it. The lie is in the claim, not the text.

## Key characters

### Leo
- ~16, logical, rejects superstition, socially isolated
- Lost his father Jack to the sickness a few years earlier, but still lives in the immediate shape of that grief
- Knows his mother only in warm blurry fragments (she died young)
- Born in exile village as the hidden continuation of Jack's bloodline
- May be the true fulfillment of the future-facing prophecy - **locked later reveal, not surfaced in active early-story material**
- Does not know his father's real name is **Rowan**

### Jack (Leo's father)
- **Birth name: Rowan** - renamed Jack by the King as a quiet act of domination. Leo doesn't know.
- The King's **younger brother** and **true bearer of the ancients gene** - the legitimate heir under the kingdom's own rules.
- Raised under a false commoner identity, believing himself an orphan the King took in. Grew up genuinely loyal and grateful.
- Rose to General in the army, replacing Richard.
- Refused the King's massacre order once he understood the mission; exiled with his squadron; rest secretly executed.
- Resisted the rebellion at first out of disbelief and fear for Leo. After Marcus was brought into that cause and refused it, the fracture between Jack and Marcus deepened; Jack eventually accepted the rebellion cause Richard had been building, then contracted the sickness. The rebellion died with his health.
- Deeply loving father. Leo was the greatest joy in his life.
- Exceptional fighter: loose, fluid, non-traditional style.

### Richard
- Former General (Jack later replaced him), one of the King's most trusted and dangerous assets.
- **Did not know** the King was Jack's brother during his service years.
- Noticed doctrinal drift and hidden wrongdoing but didn't understand the full system behind it.
- Exiled separately after Esmeralda's birth - the King noticed how Richard looked at Esmeralda and remembered Richard's connection to her mother; he removed him quietly rather than risk what he knew.
- In exile: publicly a carpenter, secretly a blacksmith. Watches over Leo per a dying promise to Jack.
- Secretly forged weapons for Jack; built outside contacts; prepared rebellion around Jack's legitimacy.
- After Jack fell ill and the rebellion failed, Richard began to fear Leo might become the last surviving path back to that stolen future, even as he remained bound by his promise to keep the boy out of open conflict. By the film opening, that fear exists, but his decision on whether Leo must ever be shaped by it is still unsettled.
- **Killed Marcus** to stop his sickness-driven attack on the town elders. The gore horrified the village; he was shunned for the spectacle rather than thanked for saving lives.
- **Esmeralda's biological father** - neither the King nor Esmeralda knows. Richard knows and watches her from afar.
- Richard and Esmeralda's mother planned to flee together. Richard pulled back, judging exposure would get them all killed. She proceeded alone and was caught and executed. He carries that.
- Murdered by Marshe in his sleep. **The sword does not reach Leo through Richard's hand** - Richard intended to give Leo Jack's sword but was killed before acting on it.
- His silence is morally compromised, not noble.

### Gideon (The King)
- Jack's **older brother**. Stole the throne by arranging the deaths of his mother and siblings. His father had already been killed in a wartime assassination during the wars with Thorne. Jack survived as an infant.
- Initially manipulated by Thorne-placed traitors into believing he was the true gene holder. **Discovered quickly he was not.** Since then his pursuit of the crystal and hold on power are entirely self-motivated - personal ambition and legitimacy fear, not the original deception.
- Suppresses the hidden doctrinal marker he cannot fulfill.
- Raised Jack as a loyal commoner soldier, deliberately.
- Forced a woman (Richard's love interest) to bear him an heir. That child was Esmeralda. The mother tried to flee; he had her executed.
- Grew genuinely attached to Esmeralda. Publicly she is Princess; beneath that public role, he is manufacturing her prophetic legitimacy despite her lacking the ancients gene.
- Keeps offscreen in early material. Composed, persuasive, system-building - not cartoonishly monstrous.
- When he later spares Leo, he does so because he sees the boy as usable - a second chance to reclaim and control what Jack became beyond his reach.

### Esmeralda
- Public daughter of the King, Princess of Eden, Leo's age, publicly adored.
- **Secretly Richard's biological daughter** - neither she nor the King knows.
- Does not carry the ancients gene. Her future claim and prophetic legitimacy are entirely manufactured.
- Sincerely believes the prophecy points to her - she is not cynically complicit.
- More alive and free-feeling outside the castle than within it.
- **Keep her parentage and connection to Richard out of active-story handling until deliberately unlocked.**
- In Act 2, she finds Leo collapsed after the Marshe death events and reports him to the King.

### Marshe
- Specific bully targeting Leo; older and physically dominant.
- Son of Marcus (Jack's former comrade, killed by Richard).
- For most of his life knew only that a man killed his father - not who specifically.
- Discovers offscreen in Act 1 that Richard was that man; the discovery itself is locked even though its exact on-page delivery is still to be placed.
- **In early stages of the sickness by Act 1**, which is loosening his restraint.
- His murder of Richard is driven by both grief/rage over his father **and** the early sickness - not wholly one, not wholly the other.
- Leo finds him at the scene, which leads into the Marshe death events.

### Marcus
- Former comrade of Jack and Richard; long carried envy toward Jack over the General rank.
- Already had a wife and young son when the massacre order came; later resented Jack because he believed Jack refused from a less personally costly position.
- Richard initially believed Marcus might support rebellion in good faith: he was battle-proven and had obvious motive to want his family out of exile.
- Marcus was brought into the rebellion plan and ultimately refused it. His refusal and resentment are part of what later erupts in the argument and fight with Jack.
- Driven into a sickness-fueled rage; attacks the town elders during a formal event; killed by Richard.
- His death cements the village's distorted view of Richard.

### Aldric
- King's man; sent to retrieve Leo after Esmeralda reports him.
- Loyal to Gideon - sincerely, not secretly conflicted.
- Threat comes from competence and conviction, not cruelty.
- Sharp observer; notices things others miss.
- **Do not introduce as antagonist from the start** - his opposition develops through later events.

### Dorian (Thorne)
- Gideon's uncle; rules Thorne.
- Made a bargain with the ancient hostile force: crystal in exchange for restored youth.
- Youth was granted early as incentive. He is truly old but appears as a strong young nobleman.
- Offscreen in Act 1.

## Key objects

### Mother's jewelry box
- Last surviving piece of Leo's mother. Deeply personal, not just valuable.
- Leo refuses to pawn it when desperate.
- Lost during the Marshe chase in Act 1.
- Realized missing only after Leo returns home, fails at training, and refuses to enter his father's room.

### Jack's sword
- Originally Jack's; now held by Richard.
- Recognizable to the kingdom (Aldric identifies it immediately when he finds Leo).
- Richard decided to give it to Leo - judged the boy was destined to rule and withholding it was the greater wrong.
- **Richard is murdered before he can act on this. The sword does not reach Leo through Richard's hand.**
- Marshe uses the sword to kill Richard. In the struggle that follows, Leo ends up fleeing with it after Marshe falls onto the blade.

### Hidden weapons cache
- Jack kept a large hidden collection of weapons in his room. Leo did not know it existed.
- Richard sometimes made new weapons for Jack to test.

### Crystal
- Real object of power; connected to awakening or reaching the ancients.
- Sought by both Gideon and Thorne (under ancient hostile force pressure).
- Its capture by compromised parties is the condition under which the buried legitimate line's future becomes possible or impossible.

---

## The sickness

- Public: escalating condition causing mental deterioration, violent outbursts, death. Publicly tied to the warlock's curse belief.
- Hidden: families sometimes killed by infected loved ones in private; population quietly shrinking; the warlock's curse belief is unwittingly close to the truth, just misattributed.
- **Deliberately introduced by the ancient hostile force** once it learned Jack's bloodline was in the village.

## Locked reveals (not surfaced in active early-story material)

- Jack's birth name is Rowan
- The King is Jack's older brother
- Jack carries the ancients gene and is the true heir
- Richard is Esmeralda's biological father
- The sickness was deliberately deployed by the ancient hostile force
- Leo may be the true fulfillment of the future-facing prophecy
- Leo's kill of Marshe must initially read as an accident; the buried instinctive skill is a later reveal
- The sword does not reach Leo through Richard's hand
- Marshe's murder of Richard is partly sickness-driven

## Key public/hidden distinctions to preserve

| Public belief | Hidden truth |
|---|---|
| Warlock's curse causes the sickness | Ancient hostile force deliberately introduced it |
| Gideon is the legitimate prophesied ruler | He cannot fulfill the hidden doctrinal marker |
| Esmeralda is Princess and the kingdom's prophesied future | She lacks the ancients gene; her future claim and prophecy role are manufactured |
| Jack was an orphaned commoner Gideon raised | Jack is Gideon's brother and the true heir |
| Richard killed Marcus out of violence | He killed Marcus to stop an attack on the town elders |
| Esmeralda is Gideon's daughter | Richard is her biological father |
