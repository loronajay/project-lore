# Adaptation Strategy

## Core recommendation

Adapt `LORE` first as a controlled visual film prototype rather than jumping straight to a full-length AI-generated feature.

The best first milestone is:
- one polished Act 1 sequence
- or a 5-12 minute proof-of-concept cut

## Why this story fits that approach

`LORE` is strongest when it is:
- intimate
- image-driven
- rhythm-driven
- grounded in fear, hunger, memory, and social pressure

That makes it a good fit for:
- highly curated still imagery
- limited animation
- short controlled motion shots
- strong sound design
- selective dialogue

It is a weaker fit for:
- long fully generated AI dialogue scenes
- highly improvised shot generation
- photoreal fake-live-action performance as the core visual identity

## Recommended visual format

Aim for:
- painterly grounded fantasy
- cinematic illustration
- limited but intentional movement
- dramatic lighting
- strong editorial control

Think in terms of:
- keyframes first
- motion second
- sound and pacing carrying much of the emotional realism

## Production philosophy

- Build film assets the way animation develops assets.
- Lock designs before generating many shots.
- Reuse environments and design packs aggressively.
- Keep early production concentrated in a small number of locations.
- Use AI as an asset and iteration engine, not as the sole director.

## First production target

The current strongest first film section is the active Act 1 run covering:
- opening dream
- market poverty and hunger
- public sickness incident
- bread and collapse
- Marshe chase and beating
- failed training
- jewelry-box loss and recovery
- Richard house reveal

Why this section works first:
- it is emotionally coherent
- it introduces the core tone
- it stays mostly inside the village
- it establishes Leo, Richard, Marshe, and Leo's father through memory
- it can be staged without needing the full kingdom yet

## Solo feasibility rules

- Prefer 6-12 second shots over complex long takes.
- Prefer reusable locations over constant new environments.
- Prefer close-up emotional storytelling over crowd-heavy spectacle.
- Keep dream sequences impressionistic rather than logistically literal.
- Use temp voice and temp score early so pacing can be tested before final assets are built.
