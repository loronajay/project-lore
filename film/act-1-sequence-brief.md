# Act 1 Sequence Brief

This document translates the current Act 1 material into production-facing sequence goals.

Use it before building styleframes, shot lists, and sequence edits.

## Scope

Current coverage:
- opening dream
- deprivation and failed market attempts
- Richard's first intervention
- public sickness rupture
- collapse and crystal dream
- Marshe chase and beating
- failed training and father-memory pressure
- jewelry-box loss and partial recovery
- Richard workshop reveal

Endpoint:
- Leo is caught inside Richard's hidden workspace after discovering the unfinished forbidden sword

## Sequence 1: Dream and deprivation

Story function:
- open with instability
- place the audience inside Leo's body before they understand his world
- establish the emotional contrast between dream violence and waking poverty

Emotional goal:
- disorientation
- fragility
- hunger
- absence

Required elements:
- battlefield fragments
- Leo waking alone
- bare house interior
- mother's jewelry box
- decorative dish

Visual emphasis:
- harsh sensory contrast between dream and waking reality
- close detail on hands, breath, sweat, worn objects
- the jewelry box as tenderness under pressure

## Sequence 2: Failed market and Richard's first intervention

Story function:
- show the village as socially cold without making it melodramatic
- establish Leo's humiliation and scarcity
- introduce Richard through action, not explanation

Emotional goal:
- shame
- need
- uncertainty

Required elements:
- market stalls
- repeated rejection
- Leo seated alone
- Richard buying the dish

Visual emphasis:
- people withholding warmth rather than openly performing cruelty
- Richard's physical weight and economy
- coins, dish transfer, and Leo's quick reaction

## Sequence 3: Bread memory and sickness rupture

Story function:
- contrast present deprivation with remembered family warmth
- reveal the sickness as physically frightening and socially destabilizing

Emotional goal:
- brief warmth
- shock
- panic

Required elements:
- bread stall
- warm family flash memory
- infected attacker
- public chaos
- Leo knocked aside

Visual emphasis:
- memory should feel warmer but not sentimentalized
- violence should read clearly and fast
- crowd fear should feel believable and contagious

## Sequence 4: Night collapse and crystal dream

Story function:
- push Leo to physical bottoming-out
- make dream material feel significant rather than random

Emotional goal:
- exhaustion
- vulnerability
- foreboding

Required elements:
- empty house at night
- rain
- collapse near the door
- crystal dream image
- mounted figure looking at Leo

Visual emphasis:
- sparse staging
- oppressive sound potential
- dream imagery should feel ancient, symbolic, and invasive

## Sequence 5: Bread return and Marshe confrontation

Story function:
- show Richard's indirect care
- bind relief to renewed pressure
- establish Marshe as a specific human threat

Emotional goal:
- brief relief
- obligation
- fear
- humiliation

Required elements:
- bread on the returned dish
- jewelry box decision
- Marshe spotting Leo
- chase
- beating

Visual emphasis:
- Leo's choice to carry the jewelry box should feel painful
- the beating should feel personal and controlled, not chaotic
- avoid turning Marshe into a stock brute

## Sequence 6: Aftermath, training failure, and father's shadow

Story function:
- deepen Leo's grief
- show his failed attempt to approach his father through imitation
- show village pressure continuing after the beating

Emotional goal:
- hurt
- frustration
- longing
- inadequacy

Required elements:
- home aftermath
- father versus Marshe's father memory
- boys throwing rocks
- pole training attempt
- father training memory
- father's closed door

Visual emphasis:
- memory should reveal emotional and physical competence in Leo's father
- Leo's training should feel earnest and painful, not comedic
- father's room should feel psychologically sealed

## Sequence 7: Jewelry-box recovery and forbidden discovery

Story function:
- move Leo from emotional loss into active search
- force him back through fear
- connect the mother's keepsake to Richard's hidden world

Emotional goal:
- panic
- dread
- partial relief
- curiosity
- danger

Required elements:
- route retracing
- refusal to re-enter the alley
- forced return
- empty recovered jewelry box
- alternate route home
- heat anomaly
- Richard's house interior
- unfinished sword
- Richard catching Leo

Visual emphasis:
- the recovered box should feel emotionally meaningful even when the jewelry is gone
- the house reveal should shift from curiosity to danger
- Richard's appearance should land as a hard stop

## Production priorities

If producing this as a proof-of-concept, prioritize these sequence blocks first:
1. Sequence 1
2. Sequence 3
3. Sequence 5
4. Sequence 7

These carry the strongest tonal identity and give the project:
- dream language
- village dread
- Leo/Marshe pressure
- Richard reveal
