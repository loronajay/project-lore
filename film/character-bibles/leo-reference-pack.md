## What the current references are telling us

### Useful from the teen references

- dark hair shape
- lean teenage proportions
- layered worn-cloth silhouette
- muted dark palette
- guarded, serious facial read
- grounded-fantasy texture direction

### Useful from the child reference

- emotional openness in the eyes
- softness in the face
- believable poverty texture
- non-glamorous vulnerability

### Current problem in the teen references

The teen images lean too strongly toward:
- armed fantasy protagonist
- stealth-adventurer styling
- competent hero silhouette
- later-story energy instead of early Act 1 fragility

That does not make them bad references.

It means they are best used as partial reference, not as direct approval for early-film Leo.

Important clarification:
- these teen references represent Leo later than the current Act 1 scope
- they should still guide his core face, hair, age read, and underlying personal identity
- they should not be used as direct wardrobe reference for present-day Act 1 village Leo

## Keep / soften / avoid

### Keep

- dark tousled hair
- youthful face
- muted, weathered clothing palette
- layered peasant / exile-village fabric logic
- grounded fantasy realism
- serious, inward expression

### Soften

- hood usage
- sharpness of posture
- visual confidence
- combat-ready silhouette
- amount of belts and gear

### Avoid for early Act 1

- visible swords on body
- assassin/rogue styling
- polished fantasy hero framing
- anything that makes him feel already battle-capable
- costume detail levels that imply status or cool-factor

## Current working interpretation

Early-film Leo should borrow:
- the core personal identity, face direction, age read, and hair direction from the teen references
- the emotional softness and vulnerability cues from the child portrait

But he should lose:
- the weapon-forward silhouette
- the heroic confidence
- the too-clean dark-fantasy protagonist vibe

And he should gain:
- poorer village-boy clothing logic
- simpler silhouette
- less gear
- less visual competence
- more material hardship in the costume

## Visual recipe for early Act 1 Leo

Use this as the current design target:

- about 16 years old
- slim from deprivation
- dark messy hair
- expressive, tired eyes
- worn village clothes
- muted browns, charcoal, weathered gray, faded earth tones
- practical rough layers
- poor-boy clothing appropriate to the exile village
- no heroic gear loadout
- no noble styling
- no visible sword in present-day village scenes

Short version:
- teen Leo's person
- poorer child-of-the-village clothing reality

## Generation targets

Generate these in order.

### Target 1: present-day portrait

Goal:
- lock face
- lock hair
- lock emotional baseline

Needed read:
- hungry
- observant
- grieving
- guarded but not numb

### Target 2: present-day full body

Goal:
- lock clothing silhouette
- lock poverty level
- remove over-designed fantasy gear

Needed read:
- teenage village boy
- worn clothing
- practical layers
- vulnerable, not heroic

### Target 3: injured Act 1 variant

Goal:
- preserve same Leo while adding damage and exhaustion

Needed read:
- same face
- same clothing family
- bruised, cut, tired

### Target 4: warm-memory variant

Goal:
- preserve identity while shifting lighting and safety

Needed read:
- same Leo
- slightly younger or softer
- warmer light
- emotional safety without losing groundedness

## Prompt language to reuse

Good direction words:
- grounded fantasy
- poor exile-village teenager
- hungry, grieving, observant
- worn rough-spun clothes
- muted earth tones
- cinematic portrait
- tactile realism
- vulnerable teenage boy
- quiet sadness

Use carefully:
- hooded
- medieval
- dramatic lighting

These can drift into over-stylized fantasy if pushed too hard.

## Prompt language to avoid

- chosen one
- prince
- assassin
- rogue
- battle-ready
- elite swordsman
- fantasy hero
- epic armor
- legendary

## First approval rubric

Approve a Leo image only if it keeps most of these true:
- reads around 16
- feels poor, not stylish
- feels specific, not generic
- face could belong to the same person across multiple shots
- emotional vulnerability is visible
- costume supports the village setting

Reject or downgrade an image if:
- he looks too old
- he looks too fashionable
- he looks too combat-ready
- he looks too beautiful in a glossy or model-like way
- the image feels like key art for a different story

## Working note on references

The child portrait should be treated as:
