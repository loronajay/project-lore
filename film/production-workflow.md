# Production Workflow

## Goal

Provide a repeatable solo process for turning `LORE` story material into finished film sequences.

## Workflow

### 1. Lock the story slice

Choose a bounded sequence from the active story docs.

Use:
- `story/world/story-guides/act-1-outline.md`
- `story/for-agents/current-handoff.md`
- `film/act-1-film-outline.md`

### 2. Build the sequence brief

For the chosen sequence, define:
- emotional purpose
- required locations
- required characters
- required props
- dream or memory needs

### 3. Create styleframes

Generate still images for:
- key beats
- hero close-ups
- environment establishes
- emotional memory images

Approve those before attempting motion.

### 4. Build a shot list

Break the sequence into:
- must-have shots
- optional enhancement shots
- shots that can remain mostly still
- shots that truly need animation

### 5. Generate motion selectively

Create short motion passes for:
- reveals
- atmosphere
- emotional pushes
- action beats

Avoid trying to animate every line of story material.

### 6. Assemble with scratch sound

Place on a timeline:
- temp voices
- room tone
- impact sounds
- temporary music

This is where pacing problems become visible early.

### 7. Revise before polishing

Fix:
- continuity
- clarity
- emotional emphasis
- shot order

Do this before spending heavily on more generations.

### 8. Finalize the sequence

Replace weak placeholders with:
- improved visuals
- better voice passes
- stronger sound design
- color and finishing adjustments

## Rule of efficiency

The more decisions you lock before generation, the better the film will look and the cheaper it will be to make.
