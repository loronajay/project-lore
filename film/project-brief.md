# Film Project Brief

## Project

- Title: `LORE`
- Target format: feature film, developed first through a proof-of-concept sequence or short
- Genre: grounded fantasy adventure
- Story mode: intimate character POV with expanding political and mythic scope

## Film goal

Create a visually striking, emotionally serious adaptation of `LORE` that preserves:
- Leo's limited point of view in Act 1
- memory-fragment storytelling
- quiet social dread
- frightening, consequential violence
- buried truth revealed through pressure rather than exposition

## Practical production goal

Build a version of the film that can be developed largely by one person using AI-assisted tools for:
- concept art
- shot development
- selective animation
- voice generation or temp voice work
- editing and sound assembly

## Recommended production model

Do not treat the first target as a full AI-generated feature.

Treat the first target as one of these:
- a short proof-of-concept built from Act 1 material
- a polished sequence reel
- a cinematic motion-comic / limited-animation film prototype

This should let the project prove:
- tone
- visual identity
- character design consistency
- pacing
- workflow viability

## What must survive adaptation

- Leo must feel human before he feels mythic.
- The village must feel socially claustrophobic.
- Richard must read as controlled, dangerous, and indirectly protective.
- Marshe must feel personal, not generic.
- Leo's father must feel warm in memory and frighteningly absent in the present.
- Dream imagery should feel important and destabilizing, not decorative.
- The film must avoid over-explaining its lore too early.

## What the film version should avoid

- generic fantasy aesthetics
- overuse of expositional narration
- fully photoreal AI live-action as the main plan
- weightless violence
- too much camera motion
- too many locations or asset demands in the first production pass
