# Dorian (King of Thorne)

## Status

- Canonical character file
- Offscreen in Act 1; background pressure on the larger world

## Story role

- King of Thorne
- Gideon's uncle and rival claimant descending from the older dynastic fracture
- Instrument of the ancient hostile force's strategy for obtaining the crystal
- Expands the crystal conflict beyond ordinary interstate rivalry into something older and more dangerous

## Public identity

- Appears as a strong, capable young nobleman
- In truth quite old — his apparent youth is a supernatural grant, not natural
- Carries the political credibility of Thorne's long dynastic grievance against Eden

## Buried reality

- Made a bargain with the ancient hostile force: deliver the crystal in exchange for restored youth
- The force granted the youth early as an incentive and to strengthen his capacity to carry out the plan
- He is already bound to the bargain through that advance payment — the youth was paid out before the crystal was secured
- Acts under pressure from a force that wants the ancients' history erased from the world
- His restored body gives him the strength, cunning, and physical credibility to pursue the plan more effectively

## Political history

- Thorne was the splintered continuation of an older shared kingdom — the split came from a long conflict between brothers
- The modern rivalry between Thorne and Eden sits on top of that older family fracture
- Fought a real early war against Eden — hard-fought on both sides, ending without resolution; the same war during which Gideon's father was killed in a wartime assassination
- After that war failed to deliver the crystal, shifted to a longer strategy: used placed traitors who had stayed behind after the original split to sabotage Eden from within for decades — radicalizing Gideon's sense of grievance, destiny, and entitlement before he took the throne
- Hoped Jack's survival and potential ability to read ancient material might eventually lead Thorne to the crystal first

## Relationships

- **Gideon**: nephew; rival; the other claimant in the interstate contest over the crystal
- **Ancient hostile force**: the power that granted his youth; the pressure behind his entire current strategy

## Locked canon

- His apparent youth is a supernatural grant from the ancient hostile force, not his natural age — keep out of active-story material until deliberately unlocked
- He is already bound to the bargain through restored youth paid out in advance
- Appears in Leo's crystal dream (Act 1) as a mounted general — a figure in armor holds the crystal upward offering it; Dorian on horseback reaches down and takes it, then turns his face toward Leo; the viewer does not know the mounted general is Dorian until deliberately revealed later
