# Leo

## Status

- Canonical character file
- Active protagonist

## Story role

- Main character
- Teenage boy at the center of the story's survival, inheritance, and perception-versus-reality threads

## Public perception

- Socially isolated
- Bullied
- Seen as weak
- Associated with his father's household and local reputation

## Current screenplay state

- Recently lost his father
- Back home after Marshe's attack
- Failed to imitate his father's style
- Refused to enter his father's room
- Realizes only afterward that he lost his mother's jewelry box during the chase

## Inner reality

- Logical and observant
- Rejects superstition
- Grieving both parents
- Loved his father very much
- Was very close with his father before the illness
- Remembers his mother only in soft, fuzzy fragments
- Carries warmth and nostalgia around those memories rather than clear detail
- Hungry, ashamed, and under pressure
- Feels inadequate compared to his father

## Act 2 arc

- Esmeralda finds Leo collapsed after the Marshe death events and returns to the castle to report a boy outside the walls
- The King scolds Esmeralda for leaving the castle, then sends the Aldric to retrieve Leo
- The General finds Leo unconscious and immediately recognizes the insignia on the sword
- Leo is brought before the King while still unconscious; the King takes time to consider what to do with him
- When Leo wakes, the King immediately begins manipulating him — telling him he once cared for Jack and raised him to be a strong man, but that his father turned his back on the kingdom, painting him as a traitor who fled duty rather than a soldier who acted on moral grounds
- The King offers to take Leo in and make him strong like his father once was
- Leo is trained and raised under the King's arrangement for approximately two years
- He is not allowed to live within the castle walls but reports to training and meetings with the King regularly
- Leo is adept at training and learns very quickly, mirroring a pattern the King recognized in Jack
- That recognition is what ultimately drives the King's decision not to let Leo join the army
- Instead the King intends to shape Leo into a personal mercenary — someone to carry out tasks meant to be done in the dark, kept close and controlled rather than elevated through the ranks
- The King treats him well otherwise and makes him feel welcomed and genuinely cared for
- Leo becomes close to Esmeralda during this period
- The King provides Leo with distinctive black hooded clothing different from everyone else — ostensibly a mark of his status, but in reality so the King's soldiers can track him at all times
- The same is done with Esmeralda, though she does not realize the purpose behind it

## Hidden significance

- Carries latent instinct and capability not yet realized
- Is unknowingly tied to a buried royal bloodline and political history
- Represents the surviving continuation of the line the King meant to erase
- May be the stronger future fulfillment of prophecy, though neither Leo nor the King understands that yet

## Relationships

- Jack (Leo's Father): deeply loved, source of grief, warmth, inheritance, and hidden mystery; Leo knows him only as Jack — the name Rowan is buried with everything else about who his father truly was
- Richard: distant protector, not yet openly trusted
- Marshe: specific bully, recurring threat, unexplained personal history, future tragedy
- Marcus (Marshe's Father): part of inherited family conflict
- Mother: source of tenderness, memory, and emotional grounding whose early death left him with only warm fragments of her
- Esmeralda: becomes a genuine close friend during the two years under the King's arrangement

## Important objects

- Mother's jewelry box, the last surviving piece of her possessions
- Jack's sword
